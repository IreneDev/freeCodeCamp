Grace Hopper
The Pioneer of Computer Science

Computer Scientist and Mathematician

Founder of first compiler A - O

"Mother of COBOL"

Author of the term "debugging"

USA Navy Rear Admiral

  • Birthplace: New York City, 1906.
  • Education: Ph.D. in Mathematics at Yale University in 1934.
  • Career: teacher of Mathematics in 1931, associate professor in 1941. After graduation at the Naval Reserve Midshipmen's School she worked on the Mark I computer programming staff headed by Howard H. Aiken at Harvard University. Hopper and Aiken coauthored three papers on the Mark I, also known as the Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator.
  • Project COBOL: since 1959 Hopper served as a technical consultant to the committee that defined the new language COBOL. She developed validation software for COBOL and its compiler as part of a COBOL standardization program for the entire Navy.
  • Standarts NIST: in 1970 Grace Hopper developed the implementation of standards for testing computer systems and components, most significantly for early programming languages such as FORTRAN and COBOL, known today as the National Institute of Standards and Technology.
  • Grace Hopper died in her sleep of natural causes on New Year's Day 1992 at her home in Arlington, Virginia; she was 85 years of age.
